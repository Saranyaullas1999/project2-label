Magic Toast

1. Divide ricotta or cream cheese among small bowls, into however many portions you need for your chosen color(s). You may want to preserve some white cheese, too.
2. Prepare your color(s), then add drop by drop to each bowl of cheese until reaching your desired color.
3. With a knife or the back of a spoon, swirl colors onto toast. Wiping down the utensil between swoops will help your colors stay distinct.
4. Embellish to your heart's content.
Sunny Day Cake
1. In a large bowl, make the pancake batter according to directions. Divide the batter equally among three smaller bowls.
2. In another small bowl, put 1 tsp of water. Sprinkle color over water and let stand for 2 minutes. With your spoon or fork, blend color and water until fully dissolved (if needed, add a little more water)
3. Drop by drop, begin adding color to each small bowl of batter, gradually increasing color until you have three distinct shades of pancake batter. If you intend to color your ganache, reserve a small amount of color.
4. Heat a griddle over medium heat, coating the bottom with a small amount of butter or oil. Working in batches, pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using about 1/4 cup each time. Brown gently on both sides.
5. Place heavy cream in a microwave-safe bowl. Heat until very hot, about 1-2 minutes. Add white chocolate chips to hot cream, and allow to stand for 5 minutes. Stir ganache until smooth, adding remaining color if using.
6. Stack your pancakes, drizzle with warm ganache, and serve

Milk and Cereal Bars
1. Line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl combine two cups of cereal. Set aside.
3. Heat cashew butter and maple syrup in the microwave or over the stovetop for 30 seconds - you want it to be fairly hot - almost boiling.
4. Carefully stir the wet mixture into the cereal. Combine thoroughly.
5. Press the combined mixture firmly into the loaf pan.
6. Prepare milk/frosting by heating coconut butter to soften and stir in coconut milk and maple syrup. Pour over the top of the cereal bars.
7. Freeze to set for 2 hours.
8. Store in your refrigerator or freezer.

Burger
1. To make the burger patty, pressure cook the carrot, peas and sweet corn for 1 whistle or until soft.
2. Add the cooked vegetables, chopped onions, red chilli powder, lemon juice, garam masala powder, salt and ginger-garlic paste to a large bowl.
3. Add lemon juice and mashed potatoes in the bowl, mix well until evenly combined. Shape the mixture into small/medium patties.
4. Now heat oil in a pan over medium flame. Roll the prepared patties in the breadcrumbs and shallow fry until golden brown on both sides.
5. Remove and keep aside.
6. Take one half of the burger bun. Spread some butter and place the lettuce on top.
7. Place the prepared vegetable patty on top. Top up with slices of onion, tomato, cucumber and cheese. If you want to make it even more delicious and healthy replace cheese with eggless mayonnaise.
8. Cover it with the other half of the burger bun. If desired, add some ketchup on top. Secure it with a toothpick if desired.
9. Serve at once with french fries. Make sure you like this recipe, rate it and let us know in the comments how it turned out to be.

Orange Juice
1. Rinse the oranges under water. Wipe them with a cloth or napkin.
2. Peel the oranges and slice them into four parts.
3. Place the sliced oranges into the grinder or juicer.
4. Drizzle some honey or add sugar and also add few ice cubes.
5. Grind until they become juicy. I don't have a pic for this step.
6. Sieve the juice over a mesh strainer. Collect the juice in a vessel.
7. Pour the collected orange juice into tall glasses. Serve the orange juice chilled and garnish with mint leaves.
